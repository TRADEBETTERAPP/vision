## 1. Prediction Market Copy Trading / Signal Following

### Comparable Market Size

- **Prediction markets overall**: $63.5B total volume in 2025, a 4× surge year-over-year (TradeTheOutcome, Mar 2026). Polymarket alone exceeded $44B in trading volume in 2025 (DeFiPrime, Jan 2026).
- **Social/copy trading platforms**: Global social trading platform market valued at $3.77B in 2028 (GlobeNewsWire), with broader estimates of $10.16B by 2026 growing to $19.81B by 2035 at 7.4% CAGR (Business Research Insights).
- **eToro** (the copy-trading pioneer): $931M revenue in 2024, $20.8B AUA, 3.73M funded accounts (eToro Q3 2025 earnings). Demonstrates the multi-billion-dollar revenue potential of copy-trading infrastructure.
- **Polymarket copy-trading ecosystem**: 170+ tools, bots, and products built around Polymarket as of Jan 2026 (DeFiPrime). Copy-trading tools like PolyCopyTrade operate with real-time wallet mirroring.

### Revenue Model for BETTER

- **Per-fill fee on copied trades**: Currently live at 2% per-fill fee on Lite Mode fills. Every signal-following trade that routes through BETTER's infrastructure generates direct protocol revenue.
- **Token-gated access**: Signal-following requires 50,000 BETTER (Lite) or 100,000 BETTER (Standard Terminal) to access, creating sustained buy-side demand.
- **Premium signal subscriptions**: Higher-tier signal channels (Whale/Apex) could command premium subscription fees or higher allocation priority.
- **Estimated revenue potential**: At $100M annual copied volume through BETTER at 2% fee = $2M/yr. At $1B = $20M/yr. Given Polymarket's $44B+ volume and growing copy-trade tooling, capturing even 0.1–1% of prediction-market copy flow is realistic.

### Token Demand Implications

- **Direct demand sink**: Every new copy-trader must acquire 50,000–100,000 BETTER to access signals.
- **Retention**: Copy-trading creates sticky behavior — users maintain positions to keep access to profitable signals.
- **Network effect**: Successful copy-traders attract followers → more holders → more volume → more fees.
- **New-holder conversion funnel**: Prediction markets are the #1 crypto growth narrative (MLB partnership with Polymarket, CFTC endorsement). New prediction-market users are a natural on-ramp to BETTER.

### Realistic Timeline

- **Live now**: Lite Mode copy trading with 2% per-fill fee is the current core product.
- **Q2 2026**: Enhanced signal-following features, wallet-basket strategies.
- **Q3–Q4 2026**: Premium signal channels with whale-tier gating.

### Key Dependencies

- Polymarket continued growth and regulatory clarity (CFTC guidance positive as of Mar 2026).
- Quality of signal generation and trader performance data.
- Competitive pressure from 170+ Polymarket ecosystem tools.

## 8. Bonded Agent Registry

### Comparable Market Size

- **Staking/security deposit models in crypto**: Total crypto staking TVL exceeds $66.86B in liquid staking alone (BingX, Jan 2026). EigenLayer (restaking) reached $15B+ TVL in 2025, demonstrating massive demand for security-deposit-based models.
- **AI agent trust/safety**: Academic research (arXiv, Mar 2026) on "Autonomous Agents on Blockchains" specifically catalogs trust boundaries and verifiable policy enforcement as critical gaps — exactly what a bonded registry addresses.
- **Agent registry precedents**: Olas (formerly Autonolas) operates an on-chain agent registry with bonding mechanics. Virtuals Protocol uses token-based agent deployment gates.
- **Insurance/slashing models**: EigenLayer's slashing model and Chainlink's staking model provide precedent for bonded collateral in autonomous systems.

### Revenue Model for BETTER

- **Bond deposit requirement**: Each registered agent must stake BETTER as collateral (e.g., 10,000–100,000 BETTER per agent depending on risk tier).
- **Registration fees**: One-time or annual registration fee in BETTER for each agent in the registry.
- **Slashing revenue**: Misbehaving agents lose a portion of their bond — slashed tokens flow to protocol treasury or affected users.
- **Insurance pool**: Optional higher bond levels that provide enhanced insurance for vault depositors interacting with bonded agents.
- **Estimated revenue potential**: 1,000 agents × 50K avg bond = 50M BETTER locked. At $0.01/BETTER = $500K locked. Registration fees at $100/agent/yr = $100K. Scales with agent count.

### Token Demand Implications

- **Permanent lock demand**: Bonded BETTER is locked for the lifetime of the agent's registration, reducing circulating supply.
- **Professional operator demand**: Agent operators are a new holder class — they need BETTER not for trading but for infrastructure access.
- **Trust signal**: Higher bonds signal more trustworthy agents, creating incentive to over-collateralize.
- **Multiplicative with agents**: Each agent × bond requirement = linear scaling of locked BETTER.

### Realistic Timeline

- **Q3–Q4 2026 (Planned)**: Basic bonded agent registry with static bond requirements.
- **H1 2027**: Dynamic bonding based on agent risk scores and track records.
- **H2 2027**: Full insurance/slashing model with dispute resolution.

### Key Dependencies

- Smart contract security for bonding/slashing mechanics.
- Agent performance scoring and monitoring infrastructure.
- Legal framework for agent liability and bond forfeiture.
- Sufficient agent ecosystem to justify registry infrastructure.

## 9. Delegation Mechanics

### Comparable Market Size

- **Liquid staking/delegation**: Liquid staking TVL exceeds $66.86B as of early 2026 (BingX). Lido alone holds $25B+ in delegated ETH. The delegation model is one of the most proven token utility mechanisms in crypto.
- **DeFi delegation**: Pendle introduced sPENDLE governance token (Jan 2026) to address delegation barriers. DeFi protocols increasingly use delegation for governance and yield optimization.
- **Copy-trading delegation**: eToro's CopyTrading system (the delegation-equivalent in TradFi) drives a significant portion of their $931M annual revenue. Users delegate capital to successful traders.
- **Restaking**: EigenLayer's restaking model ($15B+ TVL) demonstrates that delegation of staked assets to multiple services creates massive capital efficiency demand.

### Revenue Model for BETTER

- **Delegation fees**: Users who delegate their BETTER to agents/vaults/strategies pay a delegation fee (e.g., 1–5% of delegation amount annually).
- **Delegator reward share**: Delegators earn a share of the agent/vault performance, minus the delegation fee. Protocol takes a cut of this flow.
- **vBETTER (vault-share token)**: Delegated BETTER could mint a liquid derivative (vBETTER) usable in DeFi, creating additional protocol fee opportunities.
- **Estimated revenue potential**: If 100M BETTER delegated with 2% annual delegation fee = 2M BETTER/yr in fees. At scale (300M delegated) = 6M BETTER/yr.

### Token Demand Implications

- **Capital efficiency**: Delegation allows smaller holders to participate in whale-tier products by pooling their tokens — broadening the holder base.
- **Reduced selling pressure**: Delegated BETTER earns yield, reducing incentive to sell.
- **New holder segment**: Passive investors who don't want to actively trade but want yield on their BETTER holdings.
- **Network effect**: More delegation options → more attractive yield → more buyers → more delegatable capital.

### Realistic Timeline

- **Q3–Q4 2026 (Planned)**: Basic delegation to vault managers and agents.
- **H1 2027**: vBETTER liquid delegation derivative.
- **H2 2027**: Cross-protocol delegation (delegate BETTER for use across multiple venues).

### Key Dependencies

- Smart contract architecture for delegation/undelegation.
- Economic model balancing delegator returns vs. protocol fees.
- Regulatory clarity on delegation as a financial product.
- Sufficient yield opportunities to make delegation attractive.
